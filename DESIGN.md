## Overview

We Are Family Foundation (WAFF) is a global youth empowerment nonprofit co-founded in 2002 by music icon **Nile Rodgers**, inspired by the true meaning of his song "We Are Family." The visual identity must meet a high quality bar suitable for the entertainment industry (board includes CAA, Rockefeller) while remaining grounded in humanitarian mission. The brand personality is institutional yet vibrant, trustworthy yet youth-forward.

This is an RFP response — the deck must demonstrate JOIN Strategies' production quality while keeping WAFF's mission, voice, and assets front and center.

## Dual-Brand Approach

| Layer | Role | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **JOIN Strategies** | Production backbone — deck grid, typography system, accent palette | Layout structure, accent color pops, data viz, section dividers |
| **WAFF** | Hero content — logo, photography, voice, programs, stats | Cover, mission slides, program spotlights, impact numbers, closing |

Use JOIN's Limestone background as the deck default. Let WAFF's high-contrast black-and-white photography and bold messaging punch through it.

### Photography Style
WAFF's site is dominated by large-format, high-emotion photography of young people in action — summits, speaking events, community work. The photographic language is:
- **Black-and-white or desaturated** for hero/background imagery
- **Full-color** for portraits and program spotlights
- **Full-bleed** — photos run edge to edge, often with bold white or black text overlaid
- Source photography can be pulled from WAFF's Instagram (@wearefamilyfdtn) and site imagery

## WAFF Voice & Messaging

The WAFF voice is bold, declarative, and community-rooted. Copy uses the "WE" pronoun as a rhetorical device — always capitalized, always leading.

### Core Taglines
- "WE mentor. WE champion. WE amplify. WE act creatively. WE lead with purpose."
- "WE live for the power of community."
- "WE are a global family made up of different cultures, ethnicities, beliefs and backgrounds."
- "WE believe in the power of humanity."
- "WE ARE FAMILY."
- "What started as a song became a global movement..."

### Tone
- **Empowering** — youth are protagonists, not beneficiaries
- **Bold & declarative** — short punchy statements, not soft nonprofit language
- **Inclusive & global** — always emphasizing diversity of cultures, countries, backgrounds
- **Action-oriented** — "powers youth," "changing the game," "at the forefront"

## WAFF Programs (Content Pillars for Deck)

### 1. Three Dot Dash (Flagship)
Young social entrepreneurs (ages 13-19) addressing basic human needs. Inspired by poet Mattie J.T. Stepanek: "Peace is possible, when your basic human needs are met."
- **Global Teen Leaders (GTLs):** 400+ alumni from 75+ countries on 6 continents over 10+ years
- **Just Peace Summit:** Annual immersive gathering (2025: Stockholm)
- **Mentorship:** Each GTL paired with an extraordinary mentor for one year
- **Coalition partners:** UNICEF USA, Adobe, and others

### 2. Youth To The Front Fund (YTTFF)
Grants for under-30 activists fighting systemic racism, inequality, and injustice. Created in July 2020 after the murder of George Floyd.
- **110+ Frontliners funded** from 50+ countries on 6 continents
- **$1,000,000+ in grants** distributed
- **Funding partners:** EXCELerate Foundation, SAP, Sony Music, Walton Family Foundation, Warner Music / Blavatnik Family Foundation
- **2025 cohort:** "The Climate Cohort" — artivists, land stewards, fashion disruptors, cultural storytellers

### 3. Youth To The Table
Placing young leaders in policy and decision-making spaces (UN, government roundtables). 2025 highlight: UK Secretary of State Ed Miliband roundtable with WAFF climate youth leaders.

### 4. TEDxTeen
WAFF's TEDx franchise amplifying teen voices on a global stage.

## Colors

### JOIN Production Palette
The deck's structural palette — used for layout, accents, data visualization, and section differentiation.

- **Ink (#241F21):** Warm black for headlines and core text.
- **Limestone (#FCFAED):** Warm off-white page background. Softer and more organic than pure white.
- **Accents:** Orange, Gold, Lime, Green, Blue — used sparingly for highlights, CTAs, and thematic color-coding across program sections.

### WAFF Brand Palette
WAFF's own identity runs on stark black-and-white contrast layered with bold photography.

- **Black (#000000):** Logo, bold headlines, dramatic slide backgrounds.
- **White (#FFFFFF):** Reversed type on photo and black backgrounds.
- The interplay of WAFF black/white with JOIN accents creates visual energy — use JOIN color as the "pop" over WAFF's photographic foundation.

## Typography

The typography strategy leverages the elegance of **IvyPresto Headline** for narrative impact and **Graphik** for contemporary professionalism.

- **Headlines:** Set in IvyPresto Headline to establish a sophisticated and trustworthy voice. Use for mission statements and "WE..." declarations.
- **Body:** Graphik Regular ensures readability and modern clarity.
- **Impact quotes:** Large-scale IvyPresto italic for pull quotes and Mattie Stepanek-style "What if..." prompts.

## Layout & Spacing

Pitch decks follow a spacious layout with generous margins. Use the base 16px spacing scale to maintain consistent rhythm between elements. Full-bleed photography slides should alternate with clean typographic slides to create rhythm.

## Shapes

The shape language is modern and clean. Interactive elements and cards utilize a minimal **4px corner radius** (md) to feel approachable but structured. The WAFF concentric-circle motif can be used as a subtle background pattern or section divider.

## Do's and Don'ts

- **Do** prioritize high legibility for presentation environments (UN, WEF).
- **Do** use IvyPresto for "WE..." impact statements and mission declarations.
- **Do** lead with WAFF photography and youth voices — JOIN's role is invisible to the client's audience.
- **Do** use full-bleed photo slides with bold overlaid type for emotional beats.
- **Do** cite specific numbers (400+ GTLs, 75+ countries, $1M+ grants) — they're compelling.
- **Don't** use Lime or Gold for small body text — insufficient contrast against Limestone.
- **Don't** clutter slides; prioritize whitespace and single core messages.
- **Don't** let JOIN branding compete with WAFF — JOIN is the production layer, not the star.
- **Don't** use stock photography — WAFF has rich real imagery of their youth leaders.
